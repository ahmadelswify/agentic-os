# Domain Language Translation

## Why Translation Matters

The same achievement can sound completely different depending on the target company's vocabulary. A "support escalation workflow" at one company is a "customer remediation pipeline" at another. Translation is the difference between a resume that gets ignored and one that gets interviews.

## How to Translate

### Step 1: Identify the Target Domain

Read the job description carefully. Note:
- Industry-specific terms (fintech, healthtech, SaaS, marketplace)
- Role-specific vocabulary (the verbs and nouns they use)
- Metrics they care about (GMV, ARR, NPS, SLA, whatever appears in the JD)

### Step 2: Map Your Experience

For each requirement in the JD, find your matching achievement in the Impact Library. Then ask:
- What did the target company call this type of work?
- What metric would they measure it by?
- What scope would they expect?

### Step 3: Rewrite Using Their Words

| Your Language | Their Language (example) |
|---|---|
| Support ticket automation | Customer remediation pipeline |
| Escalation workflow | Incident response framework |
| Knowledge base | Self-service enablement |
| Customer health score | Account risk assessment |
| Bug triage | Issue prioritization framework |

### Translation Principles

1. **Use the JD's exact phrasing** when your experience genuinely matches. If they say "cross-functional stakeholder alignment," use those words (if that's what you actually did).
2. **Don't invent experience you don't have.** Translation changes vocabulary, not substance.
3. **Preserve your metrics.** The numbers don't change; only the framing around them does.
4. **Test readability.** After translating, read the bullet aloud. If it sounds like jargon soup, simplify.
5. **Keep a translation log.** When you find a good mapping, save it in your Learnings so you can reuse it.

## Common Domain Mappings

These patterns show up frequently across different industries:

| Generic | SaaS/B2B | Marketplace | Enterprise |
|---|---|---|---|
| Users | Customers/Accounts | Sellers/Buyers | Partners/Stakeholders |
| Revenue | ARR/MRR | GMV/Take Rate | Contract Value |
| Satisfaction | NPS/CSAT | Seller Health | Partner Satisfaction |
| Support | Customer Success | Seller Support | Account Management |
| Automation | Workflow Optimization | Platform Enablement | Process Engineering |

## When NOT to Translate

- Internal tool names that have no external equivalent (just describe what it did)
- Metrics that are already universal (percentages, dollar amounts, time saved)
- Role titles on your resume (keep the real title, translate the bullet content)
